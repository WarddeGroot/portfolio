Reducing loads of cognitive load

Ensuring a happy landing for teachers every day by simplifying the first moments of their interaction with Gynzy.

My role

UX Designer

The team

Product owner, developers

Duration

Update duration

Tools

Figma, Amplitude

The challenge

Teachers open Gynzy first thing in the morning, often while managing a classroom of arriving students. The landing experience was overwhelming — too many options, too much visual noise, and no clear path to the most important tasks. We needed to make those first moments calm, focused, and productive.

Understanding the problem

Through usage data analysis and teacher interviews, we identified the key pain points: teachers were spending too much time finding their daily schedule, frequently-used tools were buried, and the interface competed for attention with too many equally-weighted elements.

Design approach

I applied cognitive load reduction principles systematically: progressive disclosure to surface only what's needed, visual hierarchy to guide attention, and smart defaults based on usage patterns. The goal was to make the right action obvious at every moment.

Impact

The redesigned experience reduced the time-to-first-action significantly. Teacher satisfaction scores improved, and support tickets related to "finding things" decreased. Most importantly, teachers reported feeling more in control of their morning routine.
