Redesigning main navigation

Improving how users find and discover Gynzy's features and setting up a scalable navigation creating space for new product capabilities.

My role

UX Designer

The team

Product owner
3 developers

Duration

± 3 months

Tools

Figma, Amplitude, Usability testing

The challenge

Gynzy's navigation had grown organically over the years. Teachers struggled to find features, the information architecture was inconsistent, and the structure couldn't accommodate the new product capabilities being planned. We needed a navigation that was intuitive, scalable, and reduced cognitive load for busy teachers.

Research & discovery

I started by analyzing usage data in Amplitude to understand which features teachers accessed most and where they dropped off. Combined with usability testing sessions and card sorting exercises, a clear picture emerged of how teachers mentally organized their tools and content.

Before
After

Design process

Through multiple iterations and prototype testing, we landed on a simplified navigation model that grouped features by teacher intent rather than product category. This made it faster for teachers to find what they needed and created clear extension points for future features.

Impact

The redesigned navigation led to measurable improvements in feature discovery and reduced the time teachers spent looking for content. It also created the architectural foundation needed for several new product initiatives that followed.
