Setting the strategic direction

As Gynzy grew, design decisions were increasingly made in isolation — team by team, sprint by sprint. There was no shared vision for where the product experience was heading. I initiated and led a cross-team process to develop a strategic UX vision, involving stakeholder workshops, user research synthesis, and competitive analysis to define where Gynzy needed to go over the next 1–3 years.

The discovery

Through ethnographic studies in classrooms — a direct outcome of the vision work — we observed that teachers were frequently using tools outside of Gynzy for independent student practice. There was a clear unmet need: students needed a way to learn and practice independently within the platform. This wasn't something that would have surfaced from feature requests alone; it took strategic research to uncover.
