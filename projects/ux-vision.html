Crafting & Leading a Strategic UX Vision

Aligning teams to shift from doing tactical to strategic work, establishing a shared design direction that scaled across the entire product.

My role

UX Lead / Strategist

The team

Cross-functional

Duration

Ongoing initiative

Tools

Figma, Miro, Amplitude

The challenge

As Gynzy grew, design decisions were increasingly made in isolation — team by team, sprint by sprint. There was no shared vision for where the product experience was heading. This led to inconsistencies, duplicated effort, and designs that solved immediate problems without considering the bigger picture.

Creating alignment

I initiated and facilitated a cross-team process to develop a strategic UX vision. This involved stakeholder workshops, user research synthesis, and competitive landscape analysis to define where the product experience needed to go over the next 1-3 years.

From vision to execution

The vision document became a living reference that informed quarterly planning, design reviews, and prioritization decisions. It gave every team member a shared understanding of the "why" behind design decisions and helped shift conversations from "what should this screen look like" to "how does this advance our user experience goals."

Impact

The strategic UX vision created measurable alignment across teams. Design consistency improved, decision-making became faster, and the team was able to proactively identify opportunities rather than reactively solving problems.
